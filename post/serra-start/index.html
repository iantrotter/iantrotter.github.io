Kicking Off the Research Project on Stochastic Dynamic Optimisation

Ian Michael Trotter
Feb 25, 2018

We are now in the process of starting up the research project “Reverse the Curse: Can Quantum Computing Lift Bellman’s Curse of Dimensionality?”, which received financial support from Instituto Serrapilheira.

The research project combined two important topics that I have been thinking about for a long time already.

Firstly, people who know me will not be surprised to see that I continue to work on stochastic dynamic optimisation problems – also known as “stochastic dynamic programming” problems, but I prefer “optimisation”, since (mathematical) “programming” is often confused with computer programming – and to add to the confusion, my research involves both! This is a problem class that pops up absolutely everywhere, and has been a great obsession of mine ever since I first encountered it when working with valuation of natural gas storage. It was love at first sight, and currently permeates pretty much all my work. It is an incredible privilege to be able to follow my passion for (numerical) stochastic dynamic optimisation.

The surprise in this research project is the inclusion of quantum computing. The last few years we have seen amazing developments – theoretical and practical – in quantum computing. I won’t hesitate to admit that I am very new to quantum computing, my understanding of it leaves much to be desired, and the whole subject is massively intimidating. I’m sure I’m not the only one who feels that way.

However, it is an interesting frontier of research, and it is so promising that we can’t help but wonder what possibilities lay ahead. Is it possible that this new computing paradigm can help me solve larger and more complex stochastic dynamic optimisation problems than we are currently capable of handling? Or is it, perhaps, a dead end? Or perhaps somebody already did, but I don’t know about it yet? Or maybe it is even obvious for those who are more familiar with quantum computing whether it is worth pursuing or not?

So here we go, venturing into the unknown, without knowing what lies ahead. Humble about our shortcomings, our lack of knowledge and understanding, yet at the same time excited to satisfy our curiosity.

For the next year, we will work tirelessly on stochastic dynamic optimisation problems. The first step is to study state-of-the-art approximation algorithms for these problems, and attempt to make some contributions of our own. In a second stage of the project – conditional on additional funding – the deeper understanding gained from working with approximation algorithms will be leveraged, in which we aim to combine our understanding of powerful approximation methods for stochastic dynamic optimisation with quantum computing paradigms.

These days, it feels like we are at the dawn of the quantum computing era. Let’s make sure we are well prepared for it.
