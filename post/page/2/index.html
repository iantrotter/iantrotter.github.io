Two Incredibly Useful Taylor Expansions

A “Taylor expansions” is a polynomial that approximates a function in a small vicinity of a single point. They are exceptionally useful: in general, we are pretty good at solving problems involving polynomials and pretty rubbish at solving problems involving pretty much anything else. Here, we show you two elegant Taylor expansions that keep showing up here and there in different areas of research.

Everything is a Stochastic Dynamic Optimisation Problem!

Stochastic dynamic optimisation problems are everywhere – quite simply because most of the choices we make have consequences in the future, and incorporate some uncertainty: this means that the decision process needs to take into account not only whatever happens at the moment that the decision is made, but also how it may impact future decisions. These are common characteristics of real-world problems – and all such problems present a great opportunity to study the art and science of stochastic dynamic optimisation!
