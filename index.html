Biography

His research interests revolve around mathematical and computational modelling, for example of different phenomena in macroeconomics and energy economics, and he is a member of the research group on Natural and Environmental Resources.

Selected Publications

Climate Change and Electricity Demand in Brazil: A Stochastic Approach

A large number of realistic weather paths were generated based on output from a single GCM. The simulated weather paths were used to include weather uncertainty in electricity demand forecasting, in order to present a probabilistic electricity demand forecast for Brazil 2016–2100. Annual Brazilian electricity demand will peak around 2060 at about 1071–1200 TWh, and there is significant weather uncertainty, with approx. 400 TWh separating the 10th and 90th percentiles.
Ian Michael Trotter, Torjus Folsland Bolkesjø, José Gustavo Féres, Lavinia Hollanda
In Energy, 2016

Two Incredibly Useful Taylor Expansions

A “Taylor expansions” is a polynomial that approximates a function in a small vicinity of a single point. They are exceptionally useful: in general, we are pretty good at solving problems involving polynomials and pretty rubbish at solving problems involving pretty much anything else. Here, we show you two elegant Taylor expansions that keep showing up here and there in different areas of research.
